Serie A

About

Serie A is a professional league competition for football clubs located at the top of the Italian football league system and the winner is awarded the Scudetto and the Coppa Campioni d'Italia.

History

Serie A, as it is structured today, began during the 1929–30 season.

The Serie A Championship title is often referred to as the scudetto ("small shield") because since the 1923–24 season, the winning team will bear a small coat of arms with the Italian tricolour on their strip in the following season.

The most successful club is Juventus with 36 championships, followed by both Milan and Internazionale, with 18 championships apiece.

Records

Most hat-tricks in Serie A goes to Gunnar Nordahl and Giuseppe Meazza.

Most red cards in Serie A goes to Paolo Montero!
